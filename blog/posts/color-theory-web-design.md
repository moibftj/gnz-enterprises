A website's color scheme is the first sentence it speaks. Get it right and the page becomes a room one wants to sit in.

## The three jobs of color on the web
Direct attention, convey mood, organize meaning. Everything else is academia.

## The five-color palette
Paper, ink, one accent, one exception (&lt;5% usage), and a muted neutral. Semantic states (success, error, warning, info) are weather, not brand.

## 60/30/10
60% dominant neutral, 30% secondary, 10% accent. Flip these and the page feels anxious.

## Your brand is not "blue"
Nine shades of your accent (50–900). Design tools generate them in a click. Use them.

## Contrast math
4.5:1 body, 3:1 large text, 3:1 non-text UI. `#777` on `#fff` fails; use `#595959` or darker.

## Dark mode
Not inverse light mode. Background `#0F1419`–`#111827`. Text `#E5E7EB`–`#F3F4F6`. Desaturate brand 10–20% on dark canvases.

## Five working palettes for 2026
Paper white + ink + terracotta + forest; Near-black + bone + muted amber; Off-white + graphite + electric blue; Cream + umber + sage + coral; Near-black + soft white + acid lime.

## The 90-second audit
Count colors (more than seven is trouble); verify the CTA is the most saturated element; mute everything else to gray and check the CTA still pops; check body contrast; drop to grayscale and verify hierarchy holds.

## Verdict
Color is not added; it is edited down — a palette edited like a poem sells more than one mixed like a paint spill.
