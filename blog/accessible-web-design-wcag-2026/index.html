Accessible Web Design: WCAG in Plain English (and the Tactics That Actually Ship)

April 21, 202615 min readBy GNZ Enterprises

The gap between "we're committed to accessibility" and "here is the audit report from six months ago" is where the internet's bad design actually lives. In 2026, that gap is a legal liability.

Why accessibility is no longer optional

  • Law. The European Accessibility Act is in full force since June 2025. U.S. ADA lawsuit volume is climbing every year.
  • Market. ~15% of the world lives with a disability; 30–40% experience temporary or situational disability monthly.
  • SEO. Crawlers and screen readers traverse sites similarly. Accessibility work is quietly some of the best SEO work.

WCAG 2.2 without the bureaucracy

Four principles — POUR: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust. Graded A, AA, AAA. AA is the practical legal standard in 2026.

The 14 rules that cover 80% of compliance

  1. Descriptive alt text on informative images. Empty alt for decorative.
  2. Heading hierarchy that makes structural sense.
  3. Form labels for every input. Placeholder is not a label.
  4. Keyboard operability — unplug your mouse for ten minutes.
  5. Visible focus indicators. Never outline: none without a replacement.
  6. Contrast: 4.5:1 for body text, 3:1 for large text and UI components.
  7. Don't use color as the only indicator.
  8. Link text that describes the destination — never "click here."
  9. "Skip to main content" as the first focusable element.
  10. Semantic HTML over ARIA. <button>, not role="button".
  11. <html lang="en">. One line. Screen readers need it.
  12. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Pause auto-advancing carousels.
  13. Touch targets 24×24px minimum (WCAG 2.2); real target 44–48px.
  14. Error messages that explain how to fix, not just that it's broken.

The 2026 audit toolkit

  • axe DevTools — catches 30–40% of issues automatically.
  • Lighthouse — lighter, good for CI.
  • WAVE — visual overlay of issues.
  • Colour Contrast Analyser (TPGi) — eyedropper testing.
  • NVDA or VoiceOver — actual screen readers.
  • Keyboard-only testing — no tool required.

Automated tools do not make a site accessible. They remove the obvious bugs so manual testing can focus on the subtle ones.

The accessibility statement

Every serious accessible website publishes one at /accessibility: the conformance standard (WCAG 2.2 AA), known limitations, reporting process with a contact, and the date of the most recent audit. A thoughtful statement is a trust signal; a missing one paired with an inaccessible site is an evidentiary gift to plaintiffs' lawyers.

What accessibility adds to the brief

  • 4.5:1 contrast forces confident typography and palette decisions.
  • Keyboard-first makes interaction states more legible.
  • Screen-reader semantics clean up content hierarchy.
  • Reduced-motion discourages gimmicky animation.

The constraints are productive. Accessibility work is, privately, one of the fastest ways to level up as a designer.

The 2026 legal landscape

  • U.S.: ADA Title III consistently held to cover websites of public accommodations. Settlements typically reference WCAG 2.0/2.1 AA; 2026 courts trending to 2.2.
  • EU: European Accessibility Act fully in force since June 2025. Commercial sites sold in the EU must be accessible.
  • UK: Equality Act 2010 + Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations 2018.
  • Canada, Australia: Federal + state/provincial regimes. Baseline WCAG 2.1 AA, moving to 2.2.

The 30-minute accessibility sanity check

  • axe DevTools clean on top 5 pages.
  • Tab through the homepage — focus order = visual order, focus visible throughout.
  • NVDA/VoiceOver three-minute pass on the homepage.
  • Contrast check of top 5 page types.
  • Submit a form with invalid input — does the error explain the fix?
  • Mouse-unplugged primary conversion completion.
  • prefers-reduced-motion toned down?
  • Accessibility statement exists and is accurate?

Conclusion: a one-line sermon

Accessibility is not the ceiling on great design; it is the floor — and any site worth shipping in 2026 should be built so that the floor is not also the emergency exit.
